# What to Do in Radiation Emergencies

Source: CDC Radiation Emergencies. URL: https://www.cdc.gov/radiation-emergencies/response/index.html
Last reviewed: April 15, 2024

## Key points

- "Get inside" means to find a building and take shelter.
- "Stay inside" means to close and lock doors and windows and shelter in place to reduce exposure to radiation.
- "Stay tuned" means listening to emergency officials and using technology to stay informed.

## Steps to take

### Get inside

In a radiation emergency you may be asked to get inside a building and take shelter ("sheltering in place").
- Get to the middle of the building or a basement, away from doors and windows.
- Bring pets inside.

### Stay inside

Staying inside will reduce exposure to radiation.
- Close and lock windows and doors.
- Take a shower or wipe exposed parts of the body with a damp cloth.
- Drink bottled water and eat food in sealed containers.

### Stay tuned

Emergency officials are trained to respond to disaster situations and will provide specific actions to help keep people safe.
- Use radios, televisions, computers, mobile devices, and other tools to get the latest information.
- Emergency officials will provide information on where to go to get screened for contamination.
